## Description

The EPLE (Échelle Préscolaire de Littératie Émergente — Early Literacy Preschool Scale) is the first validated French-language psychometric tool for assessing [[Early Literacy]] in preschool children. Developed by researchers at [[Université de Lorraine]] (lead author: [[Aude Thomas]]), it was published in the *Journal of Research in Reading*.

## What It Measures

The EPLE covers the four core domains of [[Early Literacy]]:
1. **Letter Knowledge** — recognition and naming of letters
2. **Vocabulary** — oral vocabulary breadth
3. **Phonological Awareness** — sensitivity to sounds, syllables, phonemes
4. **Oral Comprehension** — understanding of spoken language/stories

## Significance

Before the EPLE, researchers and educators in French-speaking contexts had to use English-language tools (often translated or adapted), which are inadequate given French's distinct phonological and orthographic properties. The EPLE fills this gap.
