## Summary

A computer science paper evaluating the trustworthiness of children's stories generated by LLMs (OPT, LLaMA, Alpaca) against actual children's stories (old and modern). The study uses six story quality metrics and compares generated content against human-written baselines. For Nabojo, this paper's most useful contribution is its **quality evaluation framework** — the six criteria provide a ready-made rubric for assessing AI-generated story drafts. The finding that LLMs still fall short of human-quality children's fiction reinforces Nabojo's approach of using human authors with AI assistance rather than fully automated generation.

## Key Findings / Arguments

1. **LLMs fall short of human-quality children's fiction.** Generated stories lack the nuance, age-appropriateness, and linguistic consistency of professionally written children's stories.
2. **Six quality criteria for children's stories:**
   - *Grammar:* Grammatically correct throughout
   - *Linguistic Consistency:* Words of similar difficulty and register throughout
   - *Appropriate Language:* Age-appropriate; no offensive or overly complex words
   - *Structural Consistency:* Sentences follow similar structure; no jarring shifts
   - *Creativity:* Engaging, novel story content
   - *Adherence to Instructions:* Follows the given prompt/constraints
3. **GPT-4o-mini scores highest on grammar, appropriate language, and naturalness.** Gemma-2-9b is strongest on creativity and structural consistency. Larger models do not always outperform smaller ones.
4. **Content moderation is imperfect.** GPT-4o achieves the lowest false positive rate (mislabelling appropriate content as inappropriate) at 9%/0%, but other models have higher FPRs.
5. **The six criteria are actionable for Nabojo's pipeline QA.** Each maps to a tier-specific threshold: "appropriate language" is tier-dependent; "linguistic consistency" can be operationalised from MANULEX frequency data.

## Relevant Data

- 132 original children's stories used as baseline (old folktales + modern stories)
- Models evaluated: OPT, LLaMA-3.1-8b, LLaMA-3.1-70b, Gemma-2-9b, Gemma-2-27b, GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini
- Linguistic consistency score (0–2): top models score ~1.83–1.88 — stories are fairly consistent in register

## Limitations / Bias

- Models evaluated (2023) are already outdated; current frontier models may perform differently.
- "Children's stories" baseline includes folktales and modern Anglophone stories — not French-language content.
- Human evaluators are undergraduate computer science students, not children's literature experts.
