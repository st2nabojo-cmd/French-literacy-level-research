## Summary

An industry article on speaking speed benchmarks across contexts (presentations, podcasts, audiobooks, AI voiceover, etc.). Limited direct relevance to Nabojo's text-based pipeline, but potentially relevant if Nabojo's e-reader incorporates audio narration. The French-language speaking speed data (170–180 WPM) could inform audio narration pacing on the device. General article; not peer-reviewed.

## Key Findings / Arguments

1. **Adult speaking speed varies strongly by context:** 100–120 WPM (slow/dramatic) to 200+ WPM (auctioneer).
2. **Children's listening: 110–130 WPM with more pauses.** When speaking to children or non-native speakers, slowing to 110–130 WPM improves comprehension.
3. **Audiobooks: 150–160 WPM.** This is the professional narration standard.
4. **French is faster than English in WPM** but encodes similar information density per second — French uses more syllables to convey the same meaning.
