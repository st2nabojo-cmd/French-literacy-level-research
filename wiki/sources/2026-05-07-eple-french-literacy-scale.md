## Summary

Academic paper presenting the EPLE (Échelle Préscolaire de Littératie Émergente — French Preschool Emergent Literacy Scale), the first validated French-language tool for assessing early literacy in francophone preschoolers (ages ~4–6, pre-Tier 1). The paper identifies four domains of early literacy: letter knowledge, vocabulary, phonological awareness, and oral comprehension. Only the abstract was captured in the raw file — detailed findings, data, and implications could not be extracted.

> [!warning] This source page is based on the abstract only. The full paper (behind paywall) was not retrieved. The key_claims and findings below reflect the abstract summary, not the full paper. Confidence is therefore low. This page should be updated when the full paper is accessible.

## Key Findings / Arguments

1. **Four domains of early literacy (French context):** letter knowledge, vocabulary, phonological awareness, and oral comprehension. These are the pre-reading competencies that Tier 1 readers (ages 6–7) are consolidating at the start of Nabojo's range.
2. **No French-language early literacy assessment existed before EPLE.** This confirms a gap: most early literacy research cited for French children is either adapted from English tools or conducted in English. French-specific early literacy data is sparse.
3. **The EPLE study population is pre-Tier 1** (mean age 5.17 years, kindergarten). The findings are most relevant for understanding the entry state of Tier 1 readers, not for Tier 1 content design directly.

## Relevant Data

- Sample: **167 French preschoolers**, mean age 5.17 years (SD 0.59)
- Setting: kindergarten (maternelle)
- 4 domains assessed: letter knowledge, vocabulary, phonological awareness, oral comprehension

## Tier Implications

### Tier 1 (Manner): Pre-Tier-1 Entry State
The four EPLE domains describe what children *bring* to Tier 1. A Tier 1 story that works with children's existing competences should:
- Build on oral comprehension ability (children this age understand spoken French well above their reading level)
- Leverage phonological awareness (rhyme, alliteration, sound patterns in text add enjoyment and reinforce decoding)
- Not assume letter knowledge is complete — some Tier 1 readers (early CP) are still acquiring letter-sound pairs

> [!question] The EPLE paper's full findings (which domains predict reading outcomes at what ages, SES effects, gender effects) were not captured. These could sharpen Tier 1 Manner guidance. Priority: obtain full paper access.
