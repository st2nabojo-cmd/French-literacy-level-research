## Summary

This peer-reviewed paper from [[Université de Lorraine]] presents the [[EPLE]] (Échelle Préscolaire de Littératie Émergente — Early Literacy Preschool Scale), the first psychometrically validated French-language tool for assessing early literacy in preschool children. The study addresses a longstanding gap: while English-language assessment tools for early literacy were numerous, no equivalent existed for French-speaking contexts. Using a sample of 167 French kindergarteners (mean age 5.17 years), the researchers validated the scale across four core literacy domains and used path analysis to examine how those domains interconnect and relate to child characteristics.

## Key Findings / Arguments

1. **Assessment gap identified:** Prior to this study, no French-language tool existed to evaluate and monitor the early literacy abilities of young children in francophone contexts, despite many available English-language instruments.
2. **Four domains of early literacy measured:** The [[EPLE]] scale covers [[letter knowledge]], [[vocabulary]], [[phonological awareness]], and [[oral comprehension]] — the four domains currently recognized as the main pillars of early literacy development.
3. **Psychometric validation:** The scale was tested on 167 French preschoolers (mean age 5.17 years, SD = 0.59), all tested individually, allowing psychometric properties to be established.
4. **Path analysis of inter-domain links:** The study modelled relationships between literacy dimensions and child characteristics including sex, age, and [[socioeconomic status]] — enabling a structural picture of how these dimensions develop together.
5. **Francophone context specificity:** The authors stress that the French language has distinctive phonological and orthographic properties that make direct translation/adaptation of English tools inadequate; a purpose-built scale was needed.
